RNA Quality Control Instructions


Before submitting samples, your samples must be examined by the following four assays. You will receive a kit containing controls, tube labels, mailinglabels and instructions, which tells you how to send your samples to us.


1. Measure the optical density of total RNA and Poly A+RNA samples in TE, pH 7.5 or 8.0 including OD260, OD280, OD260 /OD280, and calculate the concentration (µg/µl) of your samples. Submit your samples with all the values above and the dilution factor that you use to take these readings. For each Poly A+RNA sample, please send us at least 5 µg in a concentration of 0.04 µg/µl.

2. Analyze 10 µg of each of your total RNA samples by running a MOPS-agarose gel* containing 1.1% formaldehyde and photograph. Please submit a photo of this result (remember to clearly label each lane with the sample label).

*gel recipe could be found at Protocol for Northern Blot Hybridization with EF1-a

3. Analyze both total and Poly A+RNA samples by Northern blot hybridization with a constitutively expressed probe*, such as EF1-a. Please submit photos or autographs of these results (remember to clearly label the name and the loading amount of each sample). Detailed protocols for Northern blotting are available at Protocol for Northern Blot Hybridization with EF1-a.


*We supply 700 pg of EF1-a mRNA for positive controls.

4. Label your Poly A+RNA samples by reverse transcription and analyze these samples by size fractionation on a gel. We recommend using the protocol of Reverse Transcription of RNA using 32P Radiolabelled Probe. The size of the 1st strand cDNA must average about 1000 bp compared to a size standard on the same gel. Please submit an autograph of this result (Remember to clearly label each lane with sample label).

¨ Please label each lane in above experiments with tube label, the loading amount of each sample and your proposal number.
