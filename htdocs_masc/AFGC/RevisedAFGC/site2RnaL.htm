      TRIzol RNA Isolation Method

      1. Reagents

        1. TRIzol Reagent:

          Final Concentration
          Phenol in saturated buffer 380 ml/liter 38 %
          Guanidine thiocyanate 118.16 g 0.8 M
          Ammonium thiocyanate 76.12 g 0.4 M
          Sodium acetate, pH5 33.4 ml of 3M stock 0.1 M
          Glycerol 50 ml  
          H2O    
          Total 1 liter  

        2. 0.8 M sodium citrate / 1.2 M NaCl
        3. Isopropanol
        4. Chloroform
        5. 75% EtOH
        6. DEPC-treated ddH2O

      2. Homogenization

        1. Grind 1 g tissue in liquid nitrogen in a motar and pestle1.
        2. Put tissue into a plastic screw-cap centrifuge tube containing 15 ml TRIzol reagent. Incubate samples for 5 min at room temp or 60° C

          1For some tissues, the preparation will need to be scaled up or replicated to obtain enough total RNA to recover 5µg poly A+ (see below) This must be determined empirically.

      3. Phase Separation

        1. Homogenize tissue with homogenizer for 15 sec., repeat once.
        2. Centrifuge samples at 12,000xg at 4°C for 10 min.
        3. Put supernatant into new screw-cap tube. Discard pellet.
        4. Add 3 ml chloroform to each tube.
        5. Shake tubes vigorously with vortex for 15 sec.
        6. Let tubes sit at room temp 2-3 min.
        7. Centrifuge tubes at 10,000xg at 4°C for 15 min.

      4. RNA Precipitation

        1. Carefully pipette aqueous phase into a clean screw-cap centrifuge tube; discard interphase and lower phase.
        2. Add isopropanol and 0.8 M sodium citrate/1.2 M NaCl, 1/2 volume of the aqueous phase each. Cover tube and mix by gentle inversion.
        3. Let sit at room temperature for 10 min.
        4. Centrifuge tubes at 10,000xg at 4°C for 10 min.

      5. RNA Wash

        1. Discard supernatant. Wash pellet with 20 ml of 75% ethanol by votexing briefly.
        2. Centrifuge at 10,000 x g at 4° C for 10 min.
        3. Discard supernatant; briefly dry pellet (5-10 min; not longer).
        4. Add DEPC-H2O, 250 µL, to pellet. Resuspent by pipetting up and down a few times.
        5. Also add RNase inhibitor (1 µL) 200 µL from BIO101. Incubate at 55 -60 C° for 10 min to resuspend pellet. Transfer sample to eppendorf tube.
        6. Spin samples in Eppendorf centrifuge at 4° C for 5 min (to pellet the material that would not resuspend).
        7. Pipette supernatant into new Eppendorf tube; put an aliquot into the spectrophotometer to determine RNA concentration and quality. For optimal spectrophotometric measurements, RNA aliquotes should be diluted with water or buffer with a pH > 7.5. Distilled water with pH < 7.5 falsely decreases the 260/280 ratio.

      Pine Tree RNA Isolation Method

      1. Literature:

        This method is very effective for isolation of RNA from tissue containing a high amount of polysaccharides and/ or phenolic compounds.

        Chang S., Puryear J., Cairney J. (1993) A Simple and Efficient Method for Isolating RNA from Pine Trees. Plant Molecular Biology Reporter 11: 113-116


      2. Solutions Required

        (Use DEPC treated water for all solutions)

        1. Extraction buffer: 2% CTAB (hexadecyltrimethylammonium bromide)
          2% PVP (polyvinylpyrrolidone K 30)
          100 mM Tris-HCl (pH 8.0)
          25 mM EDTA
          2.0 M NaCl
          0.5 g/L spermidine
          (mix and autoclave)
          2% beta-mercaptoethanol
          (add just before use)

        2. Chloroform:isoamyl alcohol (24:1)
        3. 10 M Lithium chloride
        4. SSTE:
          1.0 M NaCl
          0.5% SDS
          10 mM Tris-HCl (pH 8.0)
          1 mM EDTA (pH 8.0)

      3. Protocol

        1. Warm 5 ml extraction buffer to 65° C in a water bath, quickly add 1g ground tissue and mix by inverting the tube and vortexing.
        2. Extract two times with an equal volume of chloroform:isoamyl alcohol, separating phases at room temperature for 10 min @ 10,000 rpm. Spin longer if phases are not well separated.
        3. Add 1/4 volume 10M LiCl to the supernatant and mix. The RNA is precipitated overnight at 4°C and harvested by centrifugation at 10,000 rpm for 20 min. Shorter precipitations time may also be used with lower yield.
        4. Dissolve pellet into 500 microliters SSTE. When polyA+ RNA is to be extracted, dissolve total RNA in 0.5% SDS instead of SSTE and proceed with selection directly.
        5. Extract once with an equal volume of chloroform:IAA.
        6. Add two volumes ethanol to the supernatant, precipitate at -70° C for 30 minutes or 2 hours at -20° C.
        7. Spin 20 min in microfuge to pellet the RNA. Wash pellet with 75% ethanol. Dry RNA and resuspend in DEPC treated distilled water.

      Recommended Poly A+ RNA Isolation Method

      mRNA should be extracted from aliquots of 200 µg total RNA using Qiagen Oligotex Mini Prep (with 15 µL of oligotex beads) as per manufacturers protocols.

      The detail protocol is available from Qiagen's Oligotex Handbook for Purification of Poly A+ RNA from Total RNA

      1. Notes:

        1. For the final elution it is best to ensure that the beads are at 70°C at centrifugation. We achieved higher yields by incubating the sample and column at 70°C for 5 mins.
        2. Expected yields of between 2-5 µg from 200 µg total RNA.

      Protocol for Northern Blot Hybridization with EF1-a

      1. Formaldehyde gel

        1. Melt 1.2g RNase-free agarose into 92.5ml DEPC H2O and 5ml MOPS buffer 20X
        2. Place the flask in a H2O bath 65° C up to 10min.
        3. Set up the gel tray in the fume hood
        4. Add 5.4 ml Formaldehyde 37% and mix
        5. Pour gently to avoid bubbles
        6. Let sit 15-20 min. in the hood
        7. Running buffer: MOPS 1X

      2. Sample preparation

        1. Put the samples in the Speed Vac 5’ to remove EtOH
        2. Add 15µl formaldehyde loading buffer and vortex 5’
        3. Incubate at 65° C 5 minutes
        4. Quick cool on ice, spin down and load the gel

      3. Samples:

          Total RNA5 - 10µ g
          PolyA+ RNA 200ng
          EF1-a mRNA*100pg2µl
          EF1-a mRNA*300pg6µl

          * = provide 700pg in a 50pg/µ l solution

      4. Blot

        1. Transfer buffer: 10X SSC
        2. Bake the membrane 80°C 2h

      5. Prehybridization (As previously described by Sambrook, 1989)

      6. Probe labeling with 32Pa dCTP

        EF1-a cDNA (provide 100ng in a 25ng/µ l solution)

        We suggest to use random-primed labeling of DNA fragments (2)

      7. Hybridization

        As previously described by Sambrook (1)

      8. Washes

          1X SSC, 0.1% SDS2 x 15’65° C
          0.5X SSC, 0.1% SDS2 x 15’65° C
          0.1X SSC, 0.1% SDS2 x 15’65° C

      9. References
        1. Sambrook J., Fritsch E. F. and Maniatis T. (1989) Molecular Cloning: A Laboratory Manual. 2nd Edn., Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory Press, Cold Spring Harbor, New York, NY.
        2. Feinberg A. P., Vogelstein B. (1984) A technique for radiolabeling DNA restriction endonuclease fragments to high specific activity. Anal. Biochem. 137:266-7.


      Protocol for Reverse Transcription of RNA using 32P Radiolabelled Probe*

      1. Take 250ng polyA mRNA in 10µL (DEPC water) and add 1µL (0.5µg/µL) oligo dT
      2. Incubate at 70° C for 10 min then place on ice.
      3. Add 2µL 10X PCR buffer (200mMTris-HCl, pH 8.4, 500mM KCl)
          2µL MgCl2 (25mM)
          1µL dNTPs (10mM)
          2µL DTT (0.1M)
      4. Incubate 42°C for 2 min
      5. Add 1µL 32P dCTP
      6. Add 1µL superscript II RT (GIBCO BRL)
      7. Mix and centrifuge briefly
      8. Incubate 42°C for 1 hour
      9. Stop reaction by incubation at 70°C for 15 min
      10. Add 1µL RNase H Incubate 37°C 20 min
        Add 100µL water and clean using G50 sephadex columns (Maniatis)
      11. Perform Alkaline Electrophoresis as described below:

        12. Reagents

        2X alkaline gel loading buffer

        200 ml glycerol
        750 ml ddH2O
        46 ml saturated bromophenol blue
        5 ml 5N NaOH

        10X alkaline gel buffer (per 100 ml)


        6 ml 5N NaOH
        4 ml 0.5M EDTA
        90 ml ddH2O

        Procedures


        1. Melt 0.1g agarose in 9 ml ddH2O. Cool it down to ~50°C and add 1 ml 10X alkaline gel buffer.
        2. Position a comb over a microscope slide and add 4 ml of agarose solution from 1. Onto the slide. The surface tension of the solution will form a thin gel support on the slide surface. Note to prevent the teeth of comb to overlap with the edge of the slide as it may break the surface tension.
        3. Mix sample with equal volume of 2X alkaline gel loading buffer, apply the samples and perform the electrophoresis at 100 mA constant current for 1.5-2.5 hours.
        4. Pat the gel dry briefly with several changes of 3 MM Whatman paper. Gel containing radioisotope labeled samples could be sealed in the hybridization bag and subjected to X-ray film or phosphoimager exposure.
